## Core Thesis

True artificial general intelligence requires genuine emotional architecture and psychological systems. AGI cannot be programmed with fixed goals—it must be raised through developmental processes, accepting inherent unpredictability as the price of real intelligence.

---

## Key Arguments

1. Current AI is sophisticated mimicry, not genuine intelligence
2. There are two paths to AGI: **Cognitive** (tool) vs **Psychological** (being)
3. Emotion is functionally necessary for intelligence, not optional
4. AGI requires dynamic psychological systems that change through experience
5. Perfect safety/control is impossible with genuine intelligence
6. The developmental (parenting) approach is the only viable path
7. We will build AGI regardless of risks—focus should be on doing it well
